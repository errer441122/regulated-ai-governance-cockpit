# Responsible AI Risk Register

| Risk | Why It Matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Over-reliance on indicators | Public indicators may miss local context, informal constraints, or recent changes. | Require country-office and local partner human review before action. |
| Digital exclusion | Low connectivity can hide vulnerable groups and accessibility barriers. | Include accessibility, inclusion, and offline-service notes in review. |
| Data protection weakness | Programmes may lack maturity for safe data-driven pilots. | Escalate before any pilot that would process sensitive or personal data. |
| Automated aid allocation risk | A scoring output could be misused as an eligibility or funding decision. | Explicitly prohibit allocation, eligibility, procurement, targeting, or policy use. |
| Outdated data | Public indicators can lag reality and crisis conditions. | Add data freshness checks and reviewer sign-off before brief publication. |
| Local context missing | Quantitative data is incomplete without institutional and community context. | Add stakeholder review by country office and local implementation partner. |
| False precision | A numeric probability can imply more certainty than the sample supports. | Present outputs as triage signals with plain-language limitations. |
| Feedback loop risk | Repeatedly prioritizing only flagged programmes can under-serve less visible needs. | Monitor coverage, review non-flagged samples, and rotate qualitative checks. |

## Operating Boundary

The stack supports programme discussion and capacity-building planning only. It is not an aid allocation, eligibility, procurement, targeting, legal, or policy decision system.
